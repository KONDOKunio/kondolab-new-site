Direction, Lighting and Camera Work

Introduction to My Research Papers

This page introduces research related to lighting and camera work for visual direction. Among the many processes involved in visual content production, direction work performed by directors is considered one of the most creative and experience-dependent tasks.

For this reason, our research analyzes direction work, which has often been regarded as difficult to digitize, and explores methods for improving its efficiency through computer support. We analyze how directors use scenario information and other literal information to design artistic settings, character personalities, motion, dialogue, music, sound effects, lighting, and camera work.

We have also studied support engines that arrange such information into scenes and shots, simulate object movements and camera views, and help directors construct intended visual direction. In particular, this research focuses on digital scrapbooks for lighting design and information analysis for camera work. These methods help creators set lighting and camera work more easily in accordance with their intended direction.

Doctoral Dissertation

金子満：次世代アニメーション制作システムに関する研究，東京工業大学、 1996

Today, pre-visualization is widely used to check camera work and character motion before full production. In the 1990s, however, computer-based production support was still developing, and various methods were being proposed. Methods for checking moving scenes in advance were later incorporated into previs systems.

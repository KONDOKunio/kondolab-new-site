Computer Graphics Education Research

This page introduces major education-related research papers on computer graphics, animation, character making, CG programming, geometric modeling, CAD education, and graphic science education. In university education, educational practice is as important as research itself, and it is important to develop, organize, and improve educational methods through continuous practice.

Based on computer graphics, I have conducted various educational practices for engineering, art, and media students. This page also introduces related software, tools, libraries, and teaching materials. Lecture materials are linked from the lecture pages.

Jimmy: NPR Educational System

Javaプログラミングによる絵画調画像生成教育用システム

Jimmy is an educational system for generating painterly images using Java programming. It was developed as a system for teaching non-photorealistic rendering through 2D image processing and programming exercises.

Web-Based Training System for Advanced Computer Graphics Courses

Since the mid-1990s, we have developed Java-based teaching materials for computer graphics technology education. These materials were created using Java so that they could be published on the Internet, and they were used in various educational practices.

Professor Tomoyuki Nishita also developed many Java-based CG teaching materials and published them on the Web. Based on these activities, the CG-ARTS Association proposed the development of Web-based CG teaching materials, and we presented related research together with Professor Nishita and others. These achievements contributed to Professor Nishita receiving the 2001 IPSJ Excellent Educational Material Award.

Geometric Modeling and CAD Curriculum

形状モデリング、CAD教育

Education in geometric modeling and CAD often focuses mainly on the use of tools, while insufficient attention is paid to how different shapes are constructed. To address this issue, we proposed a systematic approach to geometric modeling education and conducted trial educational practices. These outcomes were also applied to graphic science exercises at the College of Arts and Sciences, the University of Tokyo, and contributed to the development of CG and modeling education using commercial software.

Present Status of Graphic Science Education in Japan

We conducted a nationwide survey of the actual status of graphic science education among members of the Japan Society for Graphic Science and teachers involved in graphic science education. The results were presented at international conferences on geometry and graphics, Japan-China graphics education conferences, and meetings of the Japan Society for Graphic Science Education Research Group.

  1. Kunio Kondo, Kazuko Mende, Kenjiro Suzuki, Present Status of Graphics Science and Graphics Representation Education in Japan, International Society for Geometry and Graphics, Journal for Geometry and Graphics, volume 9, No.1, pp.77-87, 2005

Game Production Technology

Introduction to My Research Papers

This page introduces research related to game production technology, real-time computer graphics, visual simulation, creative applications, and interactive content design. These studies connect computer graphics, content production, game development, and visual expression technologies.

Realtime Non-Photorealistic Rendering

Since around 2000, improvements in computer performance and the expansion of expressive styles in games have increased the demand not only for realistic rendering, but also for animation-like and manga-like visual expressions. This research addresses real-time non-photorealistic rendering methods for game and interactive content production.

Visual Simulation and Realtime CG

Many areas of game graphics require realistic visual expression. This research deals with visual simulation and real-time computer graphics. Although aurora and glazed-frost expressions were not yet fully real-time processes, the studies aimed to make such simulations faster and more applicable to games and digital content production.

Visual Simulation of Aurora

This research simulated aurora motion using a simple method for CG animation generation. Earlier techniques could simulate only simple aurora shapes and motions. This study made it possible to create more realistic aurora expressions by simulating disconnection and reconnection phenomena in curtain-like waving aurora forms.

Creative Application

This research developed from exercises in which students prototyped applications and content from the viewpoint of design, connecting culture and technology. The aim is to deeply understand both culture and technology and to design interactive content based on that understanding.
